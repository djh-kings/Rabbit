Target Identification

Subject 001

"Dr Death"  — civilian alias: Dr Jane Blythe

DESIGNATION Hostile. Bipedal. No floppy ears, no whiskers, no tail of any description. Nose remains entirely stationary at all times — do not mistake this for friendly intent; she is simply incapable of the signal.

HISTORICAL RECORD Known to operate from a fixed dwelling. The record across multiple generations attests to acts of dissection and to the consumption of our kindred. The folk-name "Dr Death" is to be used in all formal correspondence and in all whispered ones.

APPROACH PROTOCOL
Do not.

Observation only. Cuteness is not a defensive posture against this subject. She has demonstrated, across decades, full and total immunity.

Pattern of Life

Brighton → Wimbledon

Surveillance summary — collated from twelve hedgerow cells, eight rooftop observers and one extremely brave pigeon.
DOMICILE
Subject resides in the vicinity of Brighton. The precise dwelling is known to command and will not be circulated below divisional level. Coastal positioning is noted; the proximity of open water complicates pursuit and favours the subject. The local gull population is not aligned with either side and is to be treated as neutral until proven otherwise.
DAILY MOVEMENT
Subject departs the residence each weekday morning and travels by rail to Wimbledon. She returns by the same means in the evening. The journey is long. The journey is regular. The journey is, in the assessment of the General Staff, the single greatest tactical opportunity available to us.
STANDING ORDER
No rabbit is to board a train. We have lost three already. The Crown will not lose a fourth to a closing door, to a luggage rack, or to the wheels beneath the platform. Trackside observation only; hedgerows along the route are to be manned in shifts and sightings forwarded to the Asset in South London.
WIMBLEDON STN.
Considered hostile ground. Heavy human traffic, no cover, and at least two resident foxes operating from the embankment. Avoid.
